<details>
<summary><strong>What time period is scheduled for final examinations?</strong></summary>

Final examinations are scheduled during the following periods:

- Semester 1: November to December  
- Semester 2: April to May  
- Semester 3 (Summer): July  

</details>

---

<details>
<summary><strong>When are final examination timetables posted and where can I view them?</strong></summary>

Final examination timetables are usually posted during the following months:

- Semester 1: November  
- Semester 2: April  
- Semester 3: July  

Timetables can be viewed:
- On the University Examinations Notice Board  
- On e:Vision, where students can view their personalised examination timetable  

</details>

---

<details>
<summary><strong>What should I do if I am not seeing a module I registered for on my examination timetable?</strong></summary>

Students who notice missing modules or module code issues on their examination timetable should report the matter immediately to their College, Faculty, or School Examination Coordinator for resolution.

</details>

---

<details>
<summary><strong>What should I do if two of my examinations are scheduled at the same time?</strong></summary>

If you identify an examination clash, the following steps should be taken:

- Review your examination timetable as soon as it becomes available  
- Report all clashes immediately to your College, Faculty, or School Examination Coordinator  
- Ensure all clashes are reported at least seven days prior to the start of examinations  

If a clash is not resolved, students may be directed to sit the examination in a designated Clash Room on the day of the examination.

Cell phones are not permitted in the Clash Room.

</details>

---

<details>
<summary><strong>What do I need to sit an end‑of‑semester or summer session examination?</strong></summary>

Students are required to present:
- A valid Examination Card  
- A valid University of Technology, Jamaica student identification card  

</details>

---

<details>
<summary><strong>What should I do if I am sick and unable to sit an in‑course test or final examination?</strong></summary>

Students who are ill and unable to sit an in‑course test or final examination must submit a medical certificate.

Important guidelines:
- The certificate must be issued by a medical practitioner  
- The medical certificate should be submitted through the University Medical Centre  
- All documentation must be submitted within five working days of the missed examination  

Medical certificates submitted after this period will not be considered.

</details>
