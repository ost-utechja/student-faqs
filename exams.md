# Examinations – Frequently Asked Questions

This page provides information about in‑course tests and final examinations.<br>
Click a question to view the full answer.

---

<details>
<summary><strong>What time period is scheduled for final examinations?</strong></summary>

Final examinations are held during the following periods each academic year:<br><br>

- Semester 1: November to December<br>
- Semester 2: April to May<br>
- Semester 3 (Summer): July<br>

</details>

---

<details>
<summary><strong>When are final examination timetables posted and where can I view them?</strong></summary>

Final examination timetables are normally published during the following months:<br><br>

- Semester 1: November<br>
- Semester 2: April<br>
- Semester 3 (Summer): July<br><br>

Once released, timetables can be viewed in two main places:<br><br>

- On the University Examinations Notice Board<br>
- On e:Vision, where students can view their personalised examination timetables<br>

</details>

---

<details>
<summary><strong>What should I do if I do not see a module I registered for on my examination timetable?</strong></summary>

If you notice that a registered module is missing from your examination timetable, you should take action immediately.<br><br>

Report the issue to your College, Faculty, or School Examination Coordinator so that it can be investigated and corrected in a timely manner.

</details>

---

<details>
<summary><strong>What should I do if two of my examinations are scheduled at the same time?</strong></summary>

If you identify that two examinations are scheduled at the same time, follow these steps:<br><br>

- Review your examination timetable as soon as it is published<br>
- Report examination clashes immediately to your College, Faculty, or School Examination Coordinator<br>
- Ensure that clashes are reported at least seven days before the start of examinations<br><br>

If a clash cannot be resolved beforehand, you may be directed to sit the examination in a designated Clash Room on the day of the exam.<br><br>

Cell phones are not allowed in the Clash Room.

</details>

---

<details>
<summary><strong>What do I need to sit an end‑of‑semester or summer session examination?</strong></summary>

To sit an end‑of‑semester or summer session examination, students must present the following:<br><br>

- A valid Examination Card<br>
- A valid University of Technology, Jamaica student identification card<br>

</details>

---

<details>
<summary><strong>What should I do if I am sick and unable to sit an in‑course test or final examination?</strong></summary>

If you are ill and unable to sit an in‑course test or final examination, you must submit a medical certificate.<br><br>

Please note the following important guidelines:<br><br>

- The medical certificate must be issued by a medical practitioner<br>
- The certificate should be submitted through the University Medical Centre<br>
- Documentation must be submitted within five working days of the missed examination<br><br>

Medical certificates submitted after this time period will not be considered.

</details>
