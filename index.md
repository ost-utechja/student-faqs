## 📌 Admissions

<details>
<summary><strong>How do I apply?</strong></summary>

All applicants are encouraged to apply online via the University of Technology, Jamaica website.

Supporting documents include:
- Academic certificates  
- Birth certificate or certificate of citizenship  
- Marriage certificate (if applicable)  
- TRN  
- National ID (Passport, Driver’s Licence or Voters ID)

**Transcripts**
- Undergraduate (current or past UTech students): Not required  
- Postgraduate (current or past UTech students): Requested internally  
- New students from other institutions must submit transcripts

⚠️ Documents must be certified by a Justice of the Peace or Notary Public. Original documents are submitted only if requested.

</details>

<details>
<summary><strong>Is online my only option to apply?</strong></summary>

No. Applicants unable to apply online may:
- Print an application from the website, or
- Collect one from the Office of Admissions and Enrolment Management  
(Monday–Friday, 8:00 a.m. – 4:00 p.m.)

</details>

<details>
<summary><strong>If I am unable to submit my documents in person, what are my options?</strong></summary>

Prospective and returning students have **four (4) options** for submitting supporting documents to the University:

1. **Email** documents as attachments to:  
   📧 applicationdocuments@utech.edu.jm  

2. **Registered Mail or Courier** to:  
   Admissions and Enrolment  
   University of Technology, Jamaica  
   237 Old Hope Road, Kingston

<details>
<summary><strong>What is the cost of the application?</strong></summary>

Applying online is **free**.

Manual applications require a **non‑refundable fee**:
- Jamaican residents: **JA$1,000**
- Non‑Jamaicans: **US$40**

</details>

<details>
<summary><strong>When does the application period start and end?</strong></summary>

- **Start:** October 1 (or first weekday)
- **Deadlines**
  - Early Bird: December 1
  - Regular: March 31

Early application is recommended, especially for oversubscribed programmes.

</details>

<details>
<summary><strong>When will I receive a decision?</strong></summary>

- Early Bird: By **January 31**
- Regular: By **June 30**
- Summer applicants: By **March 30**

For queries: **admissions@utech.edu.jm**

</details>

## 💰 Fees, Registration & Accounts

<details>
<summary><strong>What is the Enrolment Commitment Deposit (ECD)?</strong></summary>

The ECD is a **mandatory, non‑refundable, non‑transferable deposit** required each semester before module selection.

- Local students: **JMD $40,000**
- International students: **USD $250**

</details>

<details>
<summary><strong>Are books included in tuition?</strong></summary>

No. Books are **not included** in tuition fees.

</details>

<details>
<summary><strong>Do I need to pay full tuition to sit final exams?</strong></summary>

Yes. **Full tuition payment is required each semester** to sit final examinations.

</details>

<details>
<summary><strong>How do I check my account balance?</strong></summary>

Log in to the student portal →  
**Student Tab → Available Reports → Account Balance Report**

For assistance:
📧 fincustomersupport@utech.edu.jm

</details>

<details>
<summary><strong>How long do payments take to update?</strong></summary>

Payments take **2 clear business days** (Monday–Friday) to be reflected in the system.

</details>

## 🎓 Scholarships & Sponsorships

<details>
<summary><strong>How do I apply for a scholarship?</strong></summary>

Scholarship listings are released annually.

Application periods:
- Returning students (Years 2–4): by **June 30**
- New students (Year 1): by **July 31**

</details>

<details>
<summary><strong>How long does sponsor payment processing take?</strong></summary>

Sponsor payments typically appear within **5–10 business days** after receipt.

Delays may occur if documentation is incomplete.

</details>

## 🏦 Students’ Loan Bureau (SLB)

<details>
<summary><strong>Do I still need to pay the ECD if I applied for a student loan?</strong></summary>

Yes. **All students must pay the ECD** to access module selection.

</details>

<details>
<summary><strong>Will SLB approval grant financial clearance?</strong></summary>

Yes. Once the loan status is updated on the university system, financial clearance is granted.

</details>

<details>
<summary><strong>What happens if SLB pays less than my tuition?</strong></summary>

Any shortfall becomes the student’s responsibility.

</details>

## 🏠 Accommodation

<details>
<summary><strong>Do students have to live on campus?</strong></summary>

No. Living on campus is optional.

</details>

</details>
